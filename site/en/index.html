WHAT IS GREEKLEAKS?

The first platform for whistleblowers in Greece

Greekleaks is an online platform that enables citizens to communicate and send anonymously and securely messages and/or material (documents, videos, audio, photos, etc.) or just information to Reporters United journalists.

Greekleaks was implemented by Reporters United in collaboration with EELLAK (Open Technologies Organization), which carried out the external evaluation of the project.

The purpose of Reporters United is, through greekleaks, on the one hand to provide a useful tool to those looking for a safe way to disclose issues of public interest, and on the other hand – in cooperation with other organizations in Greece and abroad – to open the public debate in the country us on the issue of public interest witnesses, journalistic ethics in dealing with leaks and legal protection of sources.

CONTACT METHODS

Choose how to send us your message at greekleaks

Choosing the appropriate method of contacting greekleaks depends on many factors: your familiarity with technology issues, the type and amount of information you wish to convey, and the identity of the person who has an interest in keeping the information confidential. In other words, trying to avoid the "watchful eye" of an employer is a different matter than trying to avoid the control of intelligence services of a modern large state.

Below we suggest four different ways to contact greekleaks and Reporters United network journalists. At this point, we must point out that the achievement of a secure and anonymous communication depends significantly on the observance of the instructions we have listed. Each method of communication has different characteristics and by extension different advantages and disadvantages. We are committed to doing whatever it takes to ensure that the information you share with greekleaks is not compromised. The choice of the appropriate communication channel for you is yours.

Mail seems to be the most outdated solution and yet it is extremely secure. The only limitation is the amount of data you can send. You can, of course, in addition to documents in paper form, put a data storage medium (eg USB stick) in an envelope. By mail you can send your message to the greekleaks PO Box:

T.Th. 13606

If you would like to address a specific reporter in the Reporters United community, you can note their name in the envelope.

What to watch out for

If you choose to communicate by traditional mail, it is advisable to take some precautions:

  • Mail your message from a public mailbox and not from an ELTA store.
  • Choose if you have the option of a prepaid envelope.
  • Do not use a sender's name or address on the envelope. If you want to let us know, you can note it in the contents of the envelope or give us a signal number to contact you.
  • Even documents in paper form can reveal information about the origin of a document, such as so-called printer tracking dots .

SecureDrop is arguably the most secure but also the least straightforward of the suggested communication methods.

It is an open source system that allows anyone to securely and anonymously communicate with Reporters United to convey information or material of public interest. It was created by the Freedom of the Press Foundation, chaired by Edward Snowden. SecureDrop has been installed and is routinely used by some of the world's largest news organizations (from the New York Times and Reuters to the Guardian and Monde ).

Reporters United is the first and only organization in Greece (including Greek media) to install SecureDrop following the Freedom of the Press Foundation protocol step by step.

Through SecureDrop's servers, anyone can securely send messages, information or even files, without the recipient (here Reporters United) knowing the identity of the sender or information such as their IP address and location . SecureDrop is designed to assign a unique "codename" to each user, with which they appear as the sender to the recipient. The only information shared with the recipient is the date and time the submitted message was sent, which includes GPG (GNU Privacy Guard) encrypted information and files sent by the user.

A prerequisite for SecureDrop to work is access to it via the TOR network. All information is transferred encrypted through the TOR software which guarantees the anonymity of the user. For added security, decryption of sent files and/or messages is done exclusively on a Reporters United computer that has never been connected to the Internet ("air-gapped"). This is how we can guarantee the safe viewing of your messages.

Instructions
  • Download and install the TOR browser from https://www.torproject.org. (TOR is a software that guarantees user anonymity).
  • From there, follow the instructions (also available in Greek) to connect to Reporters United's SecureDrop. If you are logging in for the first time, select "First Submission" and note in a secure place that only you will know, the code name that will be given to you. You will be able to use this code name if you want to log back in and resend a message or see a reply from us. In this case, the recipient will know that the message was sent by the same sender, but without knowing your identity.
  • If you are returning to Reporters United's SecureDrop, select Return Visit and then enter the code name you received when you first submitted.
  • You can now securely send us your message or any files you want (up to 500Mb/file), or both. When sent to us, data is encrypted and a secure decryption process is followed by trained members of the Reporters United team. Our team will review the message and/or files you sent us and contact you via SecureDrop. That is why it is important to keep the code name you will receive during the first submission.
  • Journalists will decrypt and read offline each message. Messages and all files submitted via SecureDrop will be deleted from the server at regular intervals.
  • We recommend that you also delete any responses you may receive from greekleaks after they have been read.
Tips
  • For even greater security, avoid using a public wifi connection (eg in a coffee shop) or a VPN (Virtual Private Network) connection to connect to SecureDrop. Avoid sending messages over an internet connection associated with your home or work.
  • Please note that the files you may send us also have associated metadata that may reveal information about the origin of the files.
  • Make sure your computer is free of viruses and malware. If your computer has a virus problem, it can compromise the security of SecureDrop's communication.
  • Accordingly, if a third party has gained access to your computer, using SecureDrop does not guarantee your safety.
  • Do not use the onion link in a browser other than TOR. And it won't work, and it will leave an electronic trail.
  • For even greater security, use Tails.
  • Finally, keep in mind that like any software, SecureDrop can have bugs and no one can ever guarantee 100% security. However, our team is committed to constantly checking and updating the software to ensure the smooth operation of the system.

Signal is a very simple to use communication platform, through which you can talk, send messages (chat) or even attached files

It features enhanced, end-to-end encryption. Unlike apps developed by big tech companies like Meta's WhatsApp or Viber, Signal comes from an independent, non-profit organization. It also uses open source, a practice that presumably increases the security of communication. Edward Snowden often speaks positively about Signal and the security it provides, even saying himself: "I use it every day and I haven't died yet."

Unlike WhatsApp and Viber, Signal is committed to not retaining communication metadata.

You can contact greekleaks by texting Signal +30 6981968309.

Instructions
  • Download the app to your phone, depending on whether it's Android or iPhone .
  • Connect Signal to your phone number.
  • When you contact greekleaks the journalist who will receive the messages via Signal will know from which phone number they were sent. However, we promise that this information will remain confidential. In any case, if you do not reveal your identity to us, we have no way of knowing it.
  • For extra security, you can activate the "disappearing messages" function that Signal has, with which messages are automatically deleted after a period of time that can be predetermined by the user.
  • Caution: Signal promises that your messages or speech will be transmitted securely, but this does not protect them in the event that someone gains access to your mobile phone. Here some additional tips to protect your phone.

Sending email to greekleaks ( greekleaks@proton.me ) can be done with a significant degree of security when done with PGP encryption.

PGP stands for "Pretty Good Privacy". It he most secure electronic mail encryption system to date, which ws the exchange of files and messages, preventing the access of the communications provider and ensuring the confidentiality of the unication.

PGP is based on the commonly accepted encryption technology "public key cryptography", which requires the use of a pair of keys (public-public / ate-private key) to conduct secure communications. According to this method, the user must encrypt his message using the recipient's public key. The recipient, in turn, can decrypt the message exclusively with his private key. In the event that one of the two keys is unknown, the above procedure fails.

If implemented correctly, the PGP method encrypts the content of messages in transit in such a way that no one but the sender and recipient can see them.

Protonmail

The technology is built into the email sending/receiving system, so ProtonMail users don't need to do anything else to encrypt their email. This is also the method that facilitates the most for those who do not have detailed technical knowledge.
  • If you do not have a Protonmail email, create an account. Then send your email from there to greekleaks@proton.me.
  • Note: Do not fill in the "Subject" field in the email you are sending. The content may remain secure, but often this field is readable. The email provider has access to the metadata of the communication, i.e. the address of the sender and recipient, the subject of the message and the date of sending. But because of PGP encryption, the provider cannot, even if they wanted to, read the content of your message.
  • For extra security you can use ProtonMail over the Tor network .

FAQ

Q&A

What is greekleaks?

Greekleaks is the first platform for whistleblowers in Greece: an online platform that enables citizens, through the open source SecureDrop system, to anonymously and securely send messages and/or material (documents, videos, audios, photos, etc.) up to 500mb) to Reporters United journalists. At the same time and taking all necessary precautions, it provides the option of secure communication through more "conventional" methods, such as via the greekleaks mailbox , sending a message to the Signal number or sending an encrypted PGP email to the press team.

What is the relationship of greekleaks with Reporters United and EELLAK?

greekleaks is a product of the collaboration between Reporters United and EELLAK (Open Technologies Organization). Journalists, developers and lawyers participate in its implementation. Regarding the technical part, Reporters United implemented the website, installed SecureDrop and activated the other communication methods, while EELLAK, as Reporters United's technology partner, carried out an external evaluation of the project.

Why did you make it?

Because simply no one else in Greece had done it until now. The paradox is that this is a widespread practice, used by the largest media in the world. And yet, no media in Greece has so far used such a platform, even though in modern journalism the role of whistleblowers (witnesses of public interest) and leaks is extremely important, as has been proven in international cases ( Panama Papers , Paradise Papers , etc.), but also of Greek interest (Lagarde list, wiretapping case).

The purpose of Reporters United is, through greekleaks, on the one hand to provide a useful tool to those looking for a safe way to disclose issues of public interest, and on the other hand – in cooperation with other organizations in Greece and abroad – to open the public debate in the country us on the issue of public interest witnesses, journalistic ethics in dealing with leaks and legal protection of sources.

How come you are the first? Why was there no similar initiative by other Greek media?

We can only speculate. They may not know that this technology exists or that it seems complicated. Maybe they have other priorities and are not interested in such a thing. It can also be frightening to think that they might get material that they would not be able to publish for reasons of self-interest!

Is communication via SecureDrop 100% secure?

Even SecureDrop, which until now is considered unbreakable, states in its FAQ on a similar question: “No, and no organization or product that promises 100% security is telling the truth. The purpose of SecureDrop is to create a significantly more secure environment for sources who want to share information than exists in normal digital channels, but there are always risks. However, every new version of SecureDrop with significant architectural changes is subject to a security audit by a reputable third-party security company .

We must remind you that regardless of your level of familiarity with the technology (the process is demanding but easy after the first time), it is important to follow the instructions we list to ensure communication. We are committed to doing whatever it takes to ensure that the information you share with greekleaks is not compromised.

Are there conflicting opinions about the reliability of these softwares?

American journalist Yasha Levine has expressed reservations at length (read features here ) about TOR (the browser that anonymizes access to the Internet) and Signal, noting that both are ventures that have been supported in the past (Signal) and are still (TOR) to be supported by US government agencies, which TOR itself admits . Levine's argument does not question the technical security of the two open-source software, but it does raise questions about how inaccessible these systems are to agencies like the NSA. However, to date there have been no reported breaches of either TOR or Signal.

Is it possible for the whistleblower to contact not Reporters United itself centrally but directly with a journalist of his choice?

Of course. In some of the proposed communication methods (PGP, Signal), the sender will have the option to communicate directly with any reporter in the Reporters United community they wish, without the mediation of Reporters United itself. Or alternatively (in all methods: mail, Signal, PGP, SecureDrop) he can send his message to a specific reporter in the Reporters United community.

Where is SecureDrop installed? Can someone gain physical access to it, jeopardizing the privacy of the whistleblower's information and identity?

The location where SecureDrop is installed is undisclosed. But even if someone – be it a hacker or a prosecutor – gained access to the computers used to run SecureDrop, there would be no repercussions. This is because the messages are encrypted and only the accredited users of the system have access to them, while the sent files are not kept on the computers. In essence, computers are empty boxes without software.

Do you post anything you get? How do you decide what to publish and what not to publish?

Certainly not. Reporters United is bound by the so-called "The Perugia Principles for Journalists Working with Whistleblowers in the Digital Age". This is a text that emerged as a result of the collaboration of 20 journalists and experts from around the world during the International Journalism Festival in Perugia, Italy in April 2018, and then consultation with the wider investigative journalism community, lawyers and academics. A summary of the Perugia Principles is available in Greek on the Reporters United website.

Reporters United and the journalists who make up its community are committed to following the Perugia Principles as a code of ethics that governs cooperation with whistleblowers and focuses on the protection of sources, but also on the general journalistic management of leaks in the context of the implementation of greekleaks.

Where will you post?

On the Reporters United website, always in Greek, often also in English. As the case may be, however, in collaboration with Greek and international media.

Who funds greekleaks? How much did it cost?

greekleaks is a fully self-funded venture of Reporters United.

What is the legal framework in Greece regarding whistleblowers?

In November 2022, with a delay of almost a year, Law 4990/2022 on the "Protection of persons who report violations of EU law" was passed in the Parliament, which in essence is an integration of Directive (EU) 2019/1937 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October 2019 (L 305). The passing of the law creates a first network of protection for public interest witnesses in Greece (whistleblowers) who report violations of EU law in the public and private sectors. However, despite the introduction of safeguards to avoid prosecution or retaliation, there are strong concerns that the bill passed does not fully cover public interest cases.

In November 2020, Reporters United, in collaboration with International Transparency Greece, Vouliwatch and 16 other organizations launched the initiative #Милиесть. In this context, a letter was sent to the Prime Minister for the formation of a strong, inclusive, progressive and modern institutional framework for the protection of whistleblowers. This is because whistleblowing has proven to be a powerful tool in preventing and combating actions that undermine the public interest. Unfortunately, the government chose to leave whistleblowers unprotected by delaying the implementation of the European directive for two whole years. Even today, after the passing of the bill, it is necessary to monitor the implementation of the relevant legislation.
